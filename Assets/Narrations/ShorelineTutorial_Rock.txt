Firstly, let's talk about the shorelining technique in the real-world. <br>
You might have already learned about this technique in your orientation and mobility training courses. If you have never learned about this before, no worry. We will explain this technique in detail to help you understand. <br>
Shorelining is a technique used to find a landmark or particular destination when you travel with a white cane. <br>
Imagine yourself on a sidewalk, and on your way back to your house. On your right hand side, it's a grass lawn. <br>
To reach your house, you need to walk on that sidewalk, towards your front for 30 feet, and then turn right to the pathway of your house. The grass lawn will end when the pathway shows up. <br>
So, the question is - what cane technique could you use to make sure you are walking toward the correct direction and turn to your right at the correct moment? <br>
Exactly! Shorelining can be used here. In our scenario, the 'grassline' between the sidewalk and grass lawn can be treated as a shoreline. You can walk parallel to the grassline to ensure that the direction is correct! <br>
To walk parallel to it, you will touch the floor in front of you with your cane. And constantly slide it from 'right to left', then 'left to right' to touch down several inches over each side of the shoreline's edge. <br>
Let's say the 'sidewalk' is made of 'stone'. If you walk along the grassline using the shorelining technique, you will feel the sliding patterns are 'stone' 'to' 'grass' and then 'grass' 'to' 'stone'. <br>
If your 'pathway' is also made of 'stone'. When you reach the place to turn to your right, you will feel the sliding pattern changes to 'stone' 'to' 'stone'. It is because your cane touches the pathway of your house. <br>
Alright, the example above is how to use the shorelining technique to walk along a shoreline in the real-world. <br>

Here is the 'second' part. I guess you might be curious why you need to do shorelining when using this App. <br>
It is because - when you are exploring a virtual room in this App, you sometimes will find a virtual object lies in between 'you' and 'your destination'. <br>
An example is - you hear the 'waypoint drum beat sound' on your right, but when you turn to your right, your cane touches a 'virtual obstacle'. <br>
When this happens, you need to find the 'edge' of that obstacle. Treating that 'edge' as a 'shoreline'. And, using shorelining to walk parallel to the 'edge' and circumvent the obstacle. <br>
In this App, you won't receive any physical feedback like what you do in the real-world. So, you will do shorelining based on pure audio feedback. <br>
Just like doing shorelining in the real world - to do shorelining in this app, you also need to constantly slide your cane from 'right to left' and 'left to right' to identify the change of sliding pattern. <br>
